Formentera is deliberately calm and low-key, with no mega-clubs to speak of. When you want a proper big night out, you simply hop over to neighbouring Ibiza, a short ferry away.

## Ibiza's world-famous clubs

Ibiza's club season runs roughly from June to September, with some venues stretching into early October. This is where the big-name DJ residencies play, so expect long nights and serious sound systems.

In Ibiza town, the legendary **[Pacha](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pacha+Ibiza)** has been the glamorous heart of the scene for decades. Out near the airport and San Rafael you will find **[Amnesia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Amnesia+Ibiza)** and the cult favourite **[DC10](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=DC10+Ibiza)**, known for its raw, daytime-into-night parties.

Down in Platja d'en Bossa, two open-air giants face each other: **[Hï Ibiza](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hi+Ibiza)** and **[Ushuaïa](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ushuaia+Ibiza)**, the latter famous for its poolside daytime sets. Over on the west coast, **[Eden](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Eden+Ibiza)** anchors the San Antonio scene.

Before the clubs open, warm up along Ibiza town's marina bars, or head to the San Antonio sunset strip, where **[Café del Mar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Cafe+del+Mar+Ibiza)** and **[Café Mambo](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Cafe+Mambo+Ibiza)** serve cocktails to a soundtrack of chill-out music as the sun drops.

## Getting there and back

Ferries run frequently between **[La Savina](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=La+Savina+Formentera)** and Ibiza throughout the summer, and in peak season a few sailings run late. But clubs run until dawn, so the return trip is the part to plan carefully. Always check the current ferry timetable and, in particular, the last boat home.

In practice you either catch a late or night crossing (available only at the height of summer) or you stay over in Ibiza and return on a morning ferry. Decide your strategy before you go out, not at 4am on the dancefloor.

## Nightlife on Formentera itself

If you would rather keep things mellow, the island delivers a gentler kind of night. On Migjorn beach, **[Blue Bar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Blue+Bar+Formentera)** and **[Piratabus](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Piratabus+Formentera)** are beloved sunset spots where the music is soft and the dress code is bare feet.

For a proper dance, the long-running **[Tipic](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Tipic+Es+Pujols+Formentera)** club in **[Es Pujols](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Es+Pujols+Formentera)** brings a touch of Ibiza energy without leaving the island, alongside a scatter of relaxed beach bars.

### Practical tips

- Buy club tickets in advance online, as door prices are steep and popular nights sell out.
- Dress up for the big clubs; they can be selective at the door.
- Plan your ferry return before you leave, and screenshot the timetable.
- Budget for a taxi at either end, as they are limited and in demand at night.

After the lights and the bass, it is a relief to come home to calm. Casa Trini sleeps up to eight, sits just 500 m from the sea near Sant Francesc and around 1.5 km from the La Savina port, making it the perfect rustic base to sleep off a big night and wake to the quiet of Formentera.
