Formentera's season is longer than many people expect. The island wakes up in **May** and stays warm and swimmable right through **October** — so you don't have to squeeze your trip into the crowded heart of summer to enjoy the beach.

## May — the season opens

May is when Formentera comes back to life. Days average a pleasant 23 °C with around nine hours of sunshine, wildflowers still colour the fields, and the beaches are gloriously quiet. The sea is fresh — about 18 °C — so swims are bracing rather than balmy, but the light, the calm and the space more than make up for it. Ferries, beach bars and restaurants are reopening for the season.

## June to August — high summer

From late June the island hits full stride. Temperatures sit between 27 and 32 °C, the nights are warm, and the sea climbs to its warmest — around 26 °C in August. This is the liveliest, busiest time: the famous turquoise shallows of **[Ses Illetes](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ses+Illetes+Formentera)** and the long sands of **[Platja de Migjorn](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Platja+de+Migjorn+Formentera)** fill up, and it pays to book ferries, cars and tables ahead.

## September — the local favourite

Many regulars will tell you September is the sweet spot. Daytime temperatures still hover around 27 °C, the sea is at its warmest after a whole summer of heating, and the July–August crowds have thinned. Long, calm swims and easy dinners without a reservation — this is the island at its most relaxed.

## October — still beach weather

Here's the part visitors underestimate: **October is still a beach month.** Early in the month days reach about 24 °C, and because the sea holds the summer's stored heat, the water stays a comfortable 22–25 °C — warm enough for easy swimming into mid-October. The light turns golden, the coves at **[Cala Saona](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Cala+Saona+Formentera)** empty out, and the island feels like it belongs to you. Some seasonal bars and shops wind down towards the end of the month, so a few things close — but the beach doesn't.

### When we'd come

- **For warm water and calm:** June, September and early October.
- **For peak buzz and the warmest nights:** July–August (book everything early).
- **For quiet and wildflowers:** May.
- Whenever you come, pack a light layer for the evenings — the sea breeze cools things down after sunset.

Casa Trini is open right across the season, just 500 m from the sea and sleeping 1 to 8 — so you can choose the month that suits you and still walk to the water.
